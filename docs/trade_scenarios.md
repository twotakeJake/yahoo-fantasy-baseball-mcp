## General Principles
1. **No deal that ages us up.** If the return skews older than what leaves, it's a no regardless of talent.
2. **Veterans as sweeteners only.** If they're offering a veteran as the centerpiece, the answer is no.
3. **C replacement is required before Cal moves.** Don't trade Cal without identifying a replacement — either via wire or a packaged deal (see Bulls scenario above).
4. **Ohtani's replacement is competitiveness.** Trading him means accepting a dip in weekly win rate while the young core develops. Only do it if the return is 2+ legitimate blue-chip pieces.
5. **The Bobby Witt straight swap is the wildcard.** Unconventional, but Witt at 25 for Ohtani at 31 is defensible purely on age curve. Worth exploring if Kilty comes knocking.
